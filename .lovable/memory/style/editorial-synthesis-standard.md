# Editorial Synthesis Standard

Applied to: `ask-astrology`, `family-pair-reading`, `family-system-reading`.

## The 3-layer pattern (MAJOR CLAIMS ONLY)

When the reading makes an important psychological claim about a person, structure it as:

1. **Human truth** — the lived, recognizable observation ("she is significantly more powerful than she lets on").
2. **Astrology underneath** — the chart signatures that explain it, named with sign + house, and how each piece contributes.
3. **Lived behavior** — what that combination actually looks like in real life.

Example claims that qualify as MAJOR: they are powerful but restrained, they hesitate, they revisit decisions, they protect harmony, they avoid directness, they absorb the room, they need freedom, they shut down, they are intense, they are hard to read, they are learning to take up space, they are protective around visibility, belonging, truth, anger, body, money, or relationship.

## What NOT to do

- Do NOT apply the 3-layer pattern to every sentence. Use it only when the claim is psychologically major.
- Do NOT add long proof paragraphs to minor transition lines.
- Do NOT repeat the same mechanism (same planet/sign/house combo) across multiple boxes.
- Do NOT flatten readings into keyword strings.
- Do NOT make psychological claims with no astrological support.
- Do NOT list astrology without translating into observable behavior.
- Do NOT reduce Saturn to fear, the 12th house to "delayed", the Nodes to vague destiny, or the 6th house to "body body body".

## Per-surface application

- **Ask narrative reading**: whole-chart life synthesis. Major signatures may be layered across the life arc. The 3-layer pattern should appear on the chart's central tensions, not on every bullet.
- **Parent/Child Portrait (family-pair-reading)**: limit to the 3–5 things that matter at the current life stage. Each item explains: what the person does, what gets misread, what is actually happening, what helps now. Do not turn this into the whole natal narrative.
- **Family System Reading**: the 3-layer pattern is used on group-level dynamics that matter, not on every cross-aspect.

## Final test

Reader should feel: "That sounds like me, and now I understand why the chart says it."

If a section names a placement but says nothing observable, it fails.
If a section makes a psychological claim with no placement underneath, it fails.
If two sections explain the same behavior through the same mechanism, the second one fails.
